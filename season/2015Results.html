Season wrap up

The third Augusta square poker season saw a marked improvement of the standard of play as regulars now understood more of the nuances of the game. Eight games of poker were to decide the championship, with the final places only decided very late into the last game.

2014 Champion Brian hands over to the 2015 champion Steve

The first game had 7 players plus a ring in descend down to Dave's shack where Ben started his season's campaign with a good win against some difficult opponents that tested both his mental ability, physical restraint and olfactory senses. Simon was the runner up with Paul gaining some extra bonus points for this 3rd place effort.

The second game returned to northern fringes of the square, with Mark L winning on home soil despite being sleep deprived after a long trek for charity purposes. Second place was awarded to Mark V with Dave taking out 3rd spot. Mark L leapfrogged Ben into first place on the ladder with Mark V moving up to third.

The third game had some Brazilian flair to the proceedings, with playings indulging in both traditional South American food and fire water shots to start off the night. A well lubed Jade took down the main prize (not sure how much recollection actually took place though), with Michael enjoying his first cash and Ben coming in 3rd. Ben regained the lead position on the ladder, followed by Mark L, and Mark V and Dave sharing third place.

The fourth game returned to Mark V's, with a new record of 11 players requiring two tables to cater for the populous. Simon was the eventual winner, with Brian in second and Mark L in third, all gaining copious amounts of points due to the increased patronage. Simon now took out the ladder top spot, followed by Mark L and Ben slipping to third spot.

The fifth game saw Steve host, and with players enjoying the slow cooked lamb, Carlos had his first win, with Ben second and host Steve third. Ben again regained top spot on the ladder, followed by Mark L, and Simon slipping from first to third.

The sixth game had Jade hosting for the first time, and with 'an English classic' nosh in the stomachs, and despite the lack of electricity, Steve finally got the win he had been seeking for, with Paul a worthy runner up and Ben in third. Ben now headed the ladder with a healthy 14 point gap to Steve who moved up 3 places to second, with Brian moving up a place to take out third spot.

The penultimate game saw players venturing to Paul's with 10 players battling out after some more amazing food. And just like buses, Steve got his second win in a row which meant he put pressure on Ben on the championship ladder. Brian also closed the gap with a second place finish, whilst Michael ended up third, despite his dominant chip position that was reduced far too quickly...much like his bottle of spirits. No changes in the top 3 placings, but Steve reduced the gap to ladder leader Ben to 5 points with Brian in third, 13 points ahead of Paul in fourth.

The final game of the season held at Carlos', turned out to be a cliff hanger with Steve needing to overtake Ben to take out the top spot. More wonderful South American pre-game food allowed the finale story to be played out, with Ben going out in 5th place (taken out by Steve via a generous helping of luck), and Steve needing to hold onto the top 2 spots to secure the seasons championship gold medal. And win he did, despite being 2nd to another excellent win by Junior, the ladder showed both Ben and Steve on the same points, but with Steve having played one less game, meant he was crowned the worthy winner. Ben who had been consistent all year ended up being pipped to the Gold, but should be proud of yet another podium finish. And Brian finished up the podium positions by taking down the bronze medal.

2015 - The year had proven that the general increase in understanding of the game is making the nights events more of battle. Different players are showing different styles, so it's up to other players to use that knowledge to try to get an edge.

Wishing everyone a wonderul festive season, and looking forward to a recommencement of the 2016 battle of the beize.
