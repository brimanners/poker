Season wrap up

The 2016 poker fourth season had 6 events for the August Square poker fraternity to frequent, with Steve the reigning champion hoping to continue the good form from the end of the previous season.

Starting the season at the customary home at Ben's saw the host take down the honours, with only Steve staying to watch him beat Brian into second spot and Simon taking out the bronze position.

Steve handing back the trophy to the 2016 champion Brian

Mark V then hosted the next event with 7 players fighting it out and Simon taking out the honours despite playing with one arm in a sling (strap-on), consigning Brian to a consecutive second place finish and Steve returning to the medal positions in third.

Six players then descended to Brian's. Mark V had been viewing late night poker on Channel 31 and played an aggressive game but unfortunately it didn't paid dividends for the whole night. Home advantage came to the fore yet again meaning Brian took out first place, followed by Paul's playing his first game of the season and Ben in third spot.

Players ventured up the hill to Mark L's, where lo and behold, the host took out the top spot again, with Brian again finishing in second and Ben in third.

The penultimate game had Steve hosting 10 players, and with bountiful points to be made, Jade had his moment in the sun beating Michael to the top spot with Brian in third.

The final game saw 8 players venture to the southern shores where Dave's shack hosted another great game, and home advantage anyone?..Dave played a solid patient game to edge Andy out for the honours, followed by Mark V in third.

Consistent finishing in five out of six games saw Brian's relentless charge to the seasons honours unable to be tapered, and takes out the gold medal spot, his second in four seasons. Dave's consistent fourth place finishes coupled with the final game win, saw his first foray into the seasons medal tables with a fully deserved silver. Ben finished the medal allocation and completes his medal collection haul with a bronze to match his previous two silvers and gold.

Wishing everyone a wonderful festive season with family and friends. Raising a glass to hopefully a wonderful 2017 and another great year of poker.
